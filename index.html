Rowing: A Compulsive Habit. What's the Balance Between Pain and Pleasure?

I've been rowing for six years. We often joke that it's a habit, a compulsive behavior, and the clinicians at the boathouse joke that we have more than our share of people who have been or should be diagnosed with OCD. I can say that rowers are the most Type A people I have ever met. And they have a very high pain tolerance. This graph shows my own personal level of pain and suffering over the course of a month of rowing. At the beginning, I haven't rowed, but that brings it's own pain and suffering. You can see here the various levels of suffering caused by race practice, but nothing beats the actual sprint race. This is followed by a bliss that can only be cause by the endorphin rush that follows torture.
